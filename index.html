Water analysis

The aim of this project is to classify whether the water with certain amount of certain elements included in it, is safe to drink or not.

    The Water Quality Analysis project aims to assess the safety of drinking water by leveraging machine learning techniques to analyze various parameters. The dataset comprises information on key water quality metrics such as pH value, surface amount, hardness, solids concentration, chloramines, and conductivity, with each data point annotated with labels indicating water safety. A range of machine learning models including Logistic Regression, K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Gaussian Naive Bayes, Decision Tree Classifier, Support Vector Classifier (SVC), Random Forest Classifier, and XGBoost Classifier were trained. Notably, the Random Forest Classifier and SVC achieved classification scores of 68.26% and 68.56%, respectively. Feature scaling using Min-Max scaling was applied to enhance classification performance and improve interpretability of results. The project focused on three primary goals:

  • 1. Classification: Categorize water samples into safe and unsafe categories based on specific chemical elements and their concentrations.
  • 2. Model Evaluation: Assess the performance of diverse machine learning classifiers in predicting water safety.
  • 3. Feature Importance: Determine the significance of each water quality parameter in classifying water safety.

    • The machine learning techniques to classify the safety of drinking water based on chemical composition. The achieved scores above 68% with Random Forest Classifier and SVC highlight their potential in predicting water safety. Additionally, feature scaling contributed to improved classification accuracy and interpretability of the results.
